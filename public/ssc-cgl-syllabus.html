SSC CGL Syllabus

SSC CGL is one of the flagship graduate-level examinations conducted by SSC. Its official notice defines a four-section Tier-I pattern and a broader Tier-II structure with multiple modules.

SSC CGL Tier-I

  • General Intelligence and Reasoning
  • General Awareness
  • Quantitative Aptitude
  • English Comprehension

Each section carries 25 questions and 50 marks. Tier-I is objective type and has negative marking for wrong answers.

SSC CGL Tier-II Paper-I

  • Mathematical Abilities
  • Reasoning and General Intelligence
  • English Language and Comprehension
  • General Awareness
  • Computer Knowledge Test
  • Data Entry Speed Test (DEST)

Section-III in Paper-I is qualifying in nature, which includes the Computer Knowledge Test and DEST.

SSC CGL Tier-I topic direction: reasoning includes verbal and non-verbal questions such as analogies, similarities and differences, space visualization, spatial orientation, problem solving, analysis, judgment, decision making and related areas. Quantitative Aptitude covers arithmetic and mathematics areas, while General Awareness focuses on current events and general knowledge connected with India and its neighboring countries.

SSC CGL Topic-Wise Syllabus

1. General Intelligence and Reasoning

  • Analogies
  • Similarities and differences
  • Space visualization and spatial orientation
  • Problem solving and analysis
  • Judgment and decision making
  • Visual memory and discrimination
  • Observation and relationship concepts
  • Arithmetical reasoning and figural classification

2. General Awareness

  • Current events
  • Everyday observation and scientific awareness
  • History
  • Culture
  • Geography
  • Economic scene
  • General policy
  • Scientific research

3. Quantitative Aptitude / Mathematical Abilities

  • Number systems
  • Percentages, ratio and proportion
  • Square roots and averages
  • Simple and compound interest
  • Profit and loss, discount, partnership
  • Mixture and alligation
  • Time and distance, time and work
  • Algebra, geometry, mensuration, trigonometry
  • Statistics and probability

4. English Language and Comprehension

  • Vocabulary and grammar
  • Sentence structure
  • Synonyms and antonyms
  • Spot the error and fill in the blanks
  • Spellings and one word substitution
  • Idioms and phrases

5. Computer Knowledge Test

  • Computer basics and CPU

  • Internet, web browsing, e-mail management

SSC CHSL Syllabus

SSC CHSL is a major higher secondary level examination and follows a two-tier structure. The official notice sets a four-part Tier-I pattern and a Tier-II format with mathematical, reasoning, English, general awareness, and computer-based modules followed by skill or typing components.

SSC CHSL Tier-I

  • English Language (Basic Knowledge)
  • General Intelligence
  • Quantitative Aptitude (Basic Arithmetic Skill)
  • General Awareness

Each part carries 25 questions and 50 marks. Tier-I is objective type with negative marking for wrong answers.

SSC CHSL Tier-II

  • Mathematical Abilities
  • Reasoning and General Intelligence
  • English Language and Comprehension
  • General Awareness
  • Computer Knowledge Test
  • Skill Test / Typing Test

The Computer Knowledge Test is mandatory and qualifying in nature, and the final practical module depends on the post.

SSC CHSL exam structure is easier to understand when seen in two layers: Tier-I checks the four core areas, while Tier-II expands those areas and then adds Computer Knowledge plus Skill Test or Typing Test based on the role.

SSC CHSL Topic-Wise Syllabus

1. English Language

  • Spot the error
  • Fill in the blanks
  • Synonyms and homonyms
  • Antonyms
  • Spellings and detecting misspelt words
  • Idioms and phrases
  • One word substitution
  • Sentence improvement
  • Active and passive voice
  • Direct and indirect narration
  • Cloze passage and comprehension passage

2. General Intelligence

  • Semantic analogy and symbolic operations
  • Number analogy and trends
  • Figural analogy and space orientation
  • Semantic classification and Venn diagrams
  • Number classification and drawing inferences
  • Punched hole and pattern folding
  • Series and embedded figures
  • Critical thinking and problem solving
  • Coding and decoding
  • Numerical operations

3. Quantitative Aptitude / Mathematical Abilities

  • Number systems
  • Percentages, ratio and proportion
  • Square roots and averages
  • Simple and compound interest
  • Profit and loss, discount
  • Partnership business, mixture and alligation
  • Time and distance, time and work
  • Algebra and linear equations
  • Geometry, mensuration, trigonometry
  • Statistics and probability

4. General Awareness

  • Environment around the candidate and its application to society
  • Current events
  • Everyday observation and scientific aspect
  • History
  • Culture
  • Geography
  • Economic scene
  • General policy and scientific research

5. Computer Knowledge Test

  • Organization of a computer
  • CPU, memory, input and output devices
  • Backup devices and ports
  • Windows Explorer and shortcuts
  • Basics of MS Word, Excel and PowerPoint
  • Internet, searching, downloading, uploading and e-mail
  • Networking and cyber security basics
